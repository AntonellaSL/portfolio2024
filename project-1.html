Women Helping Other Woman

UI Design

Student Project 2024

Capilano University - IDEA

IXD - 107 Visual Design II

Overview

Women Helping Other Woman (WHOW) is a brand I developed for a student project that revolves around the redesign of the Vancouver Women Health Collective's website. This assignment aims to improve the visual aesthetics and user experience of the website. The primary goal was to come up with an engaging design, where women are empowered, and to motivate them to either seek help or offer help. That's why throughout the page we can see calls to action that lead to these interactions.

Design and Modulation

For the design stage worked on fine-tuning the visual elements, optimizing the layout for responsiveness across different devices, and ensuring consistency throughout the website. I also implemented modules to develop the designs of the different states of the interactive elements, enhancing the user experience and functionality of the website.

Art Direction

In the art direction stage, I began by gathering inspiration and creating a mood board that reflected the essence of empowerment and support for women. This involved collecting imagery, color palettes, typography styles, and visual elements that conveyed strength, unity, and positivity. We experimented with organic shapes, bold colors, and dynamic typography to establish the visual direction for the project.

Structure and Wireframes Stage

Throughout the project, my focus remained on empowering women through impactful graphics and intuitive design, while adhering to copyright regulations and ethical considerations. The resulting website design for Women Helping Other Woman (WHOW) embodies strength, support, and solidarity, providing a digital platform for women to connect, access resources, and foster positive change.
